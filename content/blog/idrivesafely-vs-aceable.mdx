iDriveSafely and Aceable are the two most visible online traffic schools in the US. Both are legitimate, both are court-approved, and both have tens of thousands of verified reviews. But they are meaningfully different products and the right choice depends on where you received your ticket.

## The most important difference: state availability

This is the deciding factor for most drivers.

**Aceable** independently operates in **[California](/california) and [Texas](/texas) only**. In [Florida](/florida), [Ohio](/ohio), and Oklahoma, Aceable courses are actually powered by iDriveSafely's backend — they are white-label products, not independent Aceable courses.

**iDriveSafely** is approved in **all 50 states** and Washington D.C. It has been operating since 1998 and is the most widely accepted single provider in the country.

If your ticket is in a state other than [California](/california) or [Texas](/texas), iDriveSafely is your option between these two. Aceable does not independently operate there.

## User experience: Aceable wins clearly

Aceable was built from scratch in 2014 as a mobile-first product. The app is rated 4.9 on the App Store across 101,000+ reviews — a volume and rating that reflects a genuinely good product, not inflated scores. The course content is visually modern, well-produced, and updated regularly.

iDriveSafely has been operating since 1998 and it shows. The platform has been updated over the years but its DNA is a desktop web course from the early 2000s. It works reliably and certificates process quickly, but the experience is not enjoyable.

For drivers who care about the course experience, Aceable is the better product in [California](/california) and [Texas](/texas).

## Trustpilot vs Google reviews: a note on iDriveSafely

iDriveSafely has a 4.8 rating on Trustpilot (26,000+ reviews) but a 1.7 rating on Google (68 reviews). This discrepancy is notable. Trustpilot reviews are often collected immediately after course completion through automated emails — a high-satisfaction moment. Google reviews tend to be left organically, often by customers who had a problem. The Google rating likely reflects real customer service issues that the Trustpilot score does not capture.

Aceable's Google rating (4.5) is more consistent with its Trustpilot score, suggesting a more uniformly positive customer experience.
