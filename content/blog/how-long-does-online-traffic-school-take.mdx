<QuickAnswer>
Online traffic school takes between 4 and 8 hours to complete depending on your state. Florida requires the least (4 hours). California, Arizona, Ohio, and Virginia require the most (8 hours). Texas requires 6 hours. You do not have to complete it in one sitting.
</QuickAnswer>

Before enrolling in a traffic school course, most drivers want to know how long it will take. The answer depends on which state issued your ticket — not which state you live in or which school you choose. State law sets the minimum course length, and no school is permitted to offer a shorter course.

## Can I take it in multiple sessions?

Yes, in all states. Online traffic school does not have to be completed in one sitting. You can log in and out at any time and your progress is saved. The only deadline is the court's completion date, which is typically 30–90 days after you are granted permission to attend.

## Does the 8-hour requirement mean 8 hours of clock time?

Yes. State law requires that courses take a minimum amount of clock time to complete. Schools enforce this through timed modules — you cannot skip ahead, you cannot speed through content faster than the timer allows. The 8-hour requirement means 8 actual hours of your time.

Some schools have mandatory break timers built into the course. [Texas](/texas), for example, requires 5 hours of instruction and 1 hour of mandatory breaks for a total of 6 hours.

## Does California traffic school really take 8 hours?

[California](/california) requires 8 hours of instruction minimum. However, $5 Dollar Traffic School advertises course completion in under 2 hours — which appears contradictory. This is because California allows certain DMV-licensed schools to operate without mandatory time locks if the course has been specifically approved for that format. $5 Dollar Traffic School holds license E1014 under this provision. Not all California courses operate this way — most still enforce timers.

| [New York](/new-york) | Multiple providers | 6 hours (state minimum) |
| [Arizona](/arizona) | Multiple providers | 8 hours (state minimum) |

For states with mandatory timers, every approved school takes the same minimum time — there is no legitimate way to complete the course faster than the state minimum.

## What happens if I do not finish by the deadline?

If you do not complete the course by the court's deadline, the deferred or pending status of your ticket typically expires and you may be found guilty of the original violation. Contact your court immediately if you think you might miss the deadline — courts often grant extensions if requested before the deadline passes.
