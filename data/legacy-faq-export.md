## Michigan — 12 entries (12 rendering, 0 orphaned)

### 1. Who submits my certificate in Michigan?

Your provider reports completion to the Michigan SOS within one business day. For court-based dismissal, you receive a downloadable certificate to present to the court directly.

### 2. Who submits my certificate in Michigan?

Your course provider notifies the Michigan Secretary of State electronically, typically within 1–2 business days of completion. You will also receive a certificate by mail, but you do not need to submit it anywhere yourself — the school handles state reporting.

### 3. Does Michigan traffic school dismiss my ticket?

Michigan's BDIC prevents points from being added to your record and prevents the ticket from being reported to your insurer. Some courts also allow outright dismissal via BDIC. Critical: this benefit is available only ONCE in a driver's lifetime.

### 4. Does Michigan traffic school dismiss my ticket?

Michigan’s Basic Driver Improvement Course (BDIC) does not dismiss your ticket. Instead, it allows eligible drivers to avoid points being added to their record for the cited violation. You must receive an eligibility notice from the Michigan Secretary of State (SOS) before you can enroll — you cannot self-select into the program.

### 5. How often can I take the Michigan BDIC?

Eligibility is controlled by the Michigan SOS, which mails a notification when you qualify. You must complete the course within 60 days of receiving the eligibility letter. You cannot enroll without this notification.

### 6. Can I do Michigan BDIC online?

Yes. Michigan Secretary of State-approved online BDIC courses are available.

### 7. Can I do Michigan driver improvement online?

Yes. Michigan SOS-approved BDIC courses are available fully online, accessible 24/7 from any internet-connected device.

### 8. How long is Michigan driver improvement?

The Michigan BDIC is a minimum of 4 hours. All SOS-approved providers use the same 4-hour minimum.

### 9. How often can I do Michigan driver improvement?

Michigan's BDIC benefit is available only ONCE IN A LIFETIME — not on a periodic cooldown cycle. Once you have used it, you cannot use it again for any future ticket.

### 10. How long is the Michigan BDIC?

The Michigan BDIC is a minimum of 4 hours. Courses cannot legally charge more than $100.

### 11. How long is Michigan traffic school?

Michigan's Driver Improvement Program requires 8 hours of instruction. This applies to all approved formats, including online courses.

### 12. Can I do Michigan traffic school online?

Yes. Online driver improvement courses are fully approved in Michigan. You can complete the course from home through a state-approved provider.

Once per 12 months, and no more than 5 times in your lifetime. Florida law allows you to elect BDI once within any 12-month period for eligible violations.
